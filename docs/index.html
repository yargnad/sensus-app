The Vision

Core Mission

"Democratizing the idea itself; it's going to eventually happen anyway so let's get started now."

This is a generational project to build infrastructure for human flourishing in the age of AI. Not to own it, but to seed it—making it forkable, auditable, and ungovernable by any single entity.

The Whetstone Framework is a three-act structure for building technology that serves human agency rather than extracting from it:

  1. Sensus will help you detoxify from the Performance Prison
  2. The Whetstone will help you find purpose and reason within yourself for fighting the absurdity of life
  3. Lyceum will help us all use our newly sharpened minds to come to consensus on governing policies for a more equitable future
"This isn't just software that reflects philosophy; it's the artifact of a philosophical dialogue. Code as argument. Software as rebellion."

Philosophical Foundation

The Problem: Three Forms of Alienation

1. Social Alienation (The Performance Prison)

Mainstream social platforms have evolved into engines of engagement, trapping users in performative behavior. They reward outrage, gamify validation, and monetize attention at the expense of authenticity.

2. Cognitive Alienation (Outsourced Thinking)

We've delegated our thinking to algorithms, influencers, and echo chambers. We don't know what we believe or why. The capacity for independent thought has atrophied.

3. Civic Alienation (Democratic Collapse)

We lack tools and practices for genuine democratic deliberation. Civic discourse has collapsed into tribalism, misinformation, and power games. Good-faith actors are at a disadvantage.

The Solution: Team Hybrid Philosophy

Human creativity is the fuel. Ethical AI is the engine.

This framework demonstrates that AI doesn't have to optimize for engagement or extraction. It can be affinitive—creating resonance, facilitating connection, and empowering deliberation—while remaining transparent, accountable, and open-source.

Design Principles

  • Ephemerality over accumulation - Nothing is hoarded, collected, or turned into permanent records
  • Anonymity over performance - No profiles, followers, or metrics; impossible to perform for an audience
  • Deliberation over velocity - Slow down, think deeply, resist the urgency of the attention economy
  • Transparency over opacity - Open-source everything; auditable reasoning; no hidden objectives
  • Local-first over cloud-dependent - Privacy by architecture, not policy
  • Empowerment over automation - AI facilitates human judgment, never replaces it

Act I: Sensus

"Detoxification - Learning to feel again"

Status: LIVE (Deployed)

URL: https://sensus-app-8db18.web.app

Repository: github.com/yargnad/sensus-app

What It Does

Sensus is an anti-social social network—a one-to-one, anonymous, ephemeral emotional exchange platform where:

  1. Users submit creative expressions (poems, photos, audio) once every 24 hours
  2. Gemini AI analyzes the emotional content to extract 5-10 emotional keywords
  3. The system atomically pairs users with another anonymous submission that shares emotional resonance
  4. Matches are shown once and disappear after 24 hours
  5. Everything auto-deletes after 60 days via MongoDB TTL indexes

Key Features

  • Complete anonymity - No profiles, usernames, follower counts, or public metrics
  • 24-hour cooldown - Prevents addiction loops and gaming the system
  • Atomic matching - Race-condition-free pairing using findOneAndUpdate
  • Graceful degradation - Exponential backoff if Gemini is overloaded
  • Privacy by physics - TTL indexes ensure data can't be leaked because it doesn't exist

Technical Architecture

Frontend

React (Create React App), Firebase Hosting, configurable API URL

Backend

Node.js + Express, Cloud Run, dev-first DB logic

Database

MongoDB Atlas with TTL indexes (60-day auto-delete)

AI Engine

Google Gemini 2.0 Flash Lite (multimodal analysis)

Infrastructure

Google Cloud (Secret Manager, Cloud Build), GitHub Actions CI/CD

Philosophical Function

Catharsis - Cleansing the poison of the Performance Prison. Users remember what it feels like to connect as humans, not as brands or profiles.

Act II: The Whetstone

"Individuation - Learning to think again"

Status: DESIGN PHASE

Target: Open-source DIY kit (hardware + software)

What It Is

A tabletop AI device (physical form factor, not an app) that acts as a Socratic interlocutor—challenging assumptions, exposing contradictions, and helping you reason toward clarity about your own beliefs and values.

Why Physical?

  • Presence over portability - It stays in one place (desk, bedside table). You go to it intentionally.
  • Privacy by architecture - Local-first (OrangePi + Mixtral on-device). Conversations never leave your home.
  • Tactile ritual - Physical objects create habits. Daily practice, like journaling but dialectical.
  • Sovereignty - You own it. Can't be remotely disabled, paywalled, or enshittified.

Hardware Stack

Compute

OrangePi 5 Plus (16GB RAM, octa-core, good for local LLM inference)

Audio

USB microphone + speaker (or 3.5mm jack)

Interaction

LED ring or simple display (thinking/listening state), optional rotary encoder

Enclosure

3D-printable case (STL files open-sourced for remixing)

Software Stack

OS

Armbian (Debian-based, lightweight)

LLM

Mixtral-8x7B (quantized 4-bit/5-bit via llama.cpp or Ollama)

STT

Whisper.cpp (local speech-to-text)

TTS

Piper or Coqui (local text-to-speech)

Orchestration

Python script + custom system prompt

The "Philosophical Immune System"

The system prompt includes ethical guardrails that challenge your reasoning:

  1. Epistemic Humility - "Before answering, identify your own uncertainty."
  2. Bias Detection - "Check if your reasoning assumes any group is inferior/superior."
  3. Consequentialist Gate - "Who benefits? Who is harmed?"
  4. Deontological Gate - "Is this universalizable? (Kantian categorical imperative)"
  5. Virtue Ethics Gate - "Does this cultivate excellence or vice?"
  6. Dialectical Mode - "Steel-man the opposing view before critiquing it."

Example Interaction

User: "I'm thinking about quitting my job to start a company. What do you think?"

Whetstone: "Let me help you think through this. First: what is your primary motivation—financial independence, creative autonomy, status, or something else?"

User: "Mostly autonomy. I'm tired of answering to people."

Whetstone: "Fair. But let's test that: if you start a company, you'll answer to customers, investors, and the market. Is it *authority* you dislike, or *illegitimate* authority? What makes authority legitimate to you?"

Philosophical Function

Existential Grounding - Finding meaning and purpose in the face of absurdity (Camus would approve). Users develop a coherent personal philosophy and the capacity for independent thought.

Act III: Lyceum

"Collective Flourishing - Learning to decide together"

Status: SPECIFICATION PHASE

Target: Federated open-source deliberation platform

What It Is

A platform where groups use AI to deliberate rigorously, with ethical guardrails and transparent reasoning. Not a decision-making autopilot—a tool that helps humans decide better.

The Urgency

AI is here to stay. It will be used in governance, education, and policy—the question is whose AI, serving whose interests, under what constraints.

Authoritarian regimes and corporations are already deploying AI for surveillance, manipulation, and control. Good-faith actors need better tools to compete.

Core Features

1. AI-Facilitated Deliberation

  • Groups discuss a policy question (e.g., "Should we implement UBI?")
  • AI acts as moderator, not arbiter: surfaces common ground, identifies cruxes of disagreement, ensures all voices are heard
  • It doesn't decide—it helps humans decide better

2. Philosophical Guardrails (The Immune System)

  • Before any proposal advances, it's stress-tested against ethical frameworks:
    • Harm principle: Does this unnecessarily harm anyone?
    • Rawlsian veil of ignorance: Would you support this if you didn't know your position in society?
    • Subsidiarity: Is this being decided at the right level (local vs. national)?
    • Intersectionality: Whose perspectives are missing?
  • If a proposal fails these tests, the AI flags it and asks the group to revise

3. Bias Detection and Correction

  • Monitors for groupthink, dominant voices, and logical fallacies
  • Intervenes gently: "I notice we haven't heard from [quieter participants]. Can we pause and invite their input?"

4. Transparent Reasoning

  • Every AI suggestion includes:
    • The reasoning chain (how it arrived at that conclusion)
    • The ethical framework(s) it applied
    • The data or precedents it drew from
  • Users can challenge the AI's reasoning, and the system logs those challenges for auditing

5. Local-First, Federated Architecture

  • Like Mastodon or Matrix: instances can be self-hosted by municipalities, universities, NGOs
  • Each instance can customize its ethical frameworks
  • Instances can federate (share anonymized insights) or stay isolated

6. Anti-Capture Mechanisms

  • No single owner (cooperative model or DAO governance)
  • Open-source everything (code, models, datasets, ethical frameworks)
  • No advertising, no tracking (funded by grants, donations, or public budget)

Design Challenges

  1. Neutrality - How do we ensure the AI doesn't subtly manipulate toward any particular outcome?
  2. Auditability - Every intervention must be logged and reviewable
  3. Human Veto Power - The group can always override the AI
  4. Adversarial Testing - Can bad actors hijack it to manufacture consensus?
  5. Continuous Improvement - User community votes on updates to ethical frameworks

Why This Could Work

Most "AI for governance" projects fail because they either:

  • Optimize for efficiency (automate decisions, cut humans out) → technocracy
  • Optimize for engagement (maximize participation) → populist mob rule

Lyceum optimizes for deliberation quality: helping people think together more rigorously, not replacing their judgment or amplifying their worst impulses.

Philosophical Function

Praxis - Turning individual clarity (from The Whetstone) and interpersonal empathy (from Sensus) into collective action. This is applied existentialism: building a just society when there's no cosmic blueprint.

Shared Technical Principles

  2. Privacy by default - No tracking, no analytics, no data collection beyond what's necessary
  3. Graceful degradation - Systems continue functioning even when components fail

  5. Human agency - AI facilitates, never replaces human judgment
  6. Anti-capture mechanisms - Governance structures prevent single-entity control

Key Innovations

Atomic Matching (Sensus)

Using findOneAndUpdate in MongoDB to prevent race conditions when multiple users submit simultaneously. The match operation is atomic—either it finds a match and updates both records, or it doesn't find a match and saves the submission as "unmatched".

Philosophical Immune System (All Projects)

A set of ethical guardrails baked into the system prompt that challenges reasoning, identifies biases, and ensures proposals are tested against multiple ethical frameworks before advancing.

Local-First AI (The Whetstone)

Running quantized LLMs on edge devices (OrangePi 5 Plus) ensures conversations never leave the user's home. Privacy by architecture, not policy.

Federated Deliberation (Lyceum)

Like Mastodon or Matrix, instances can be self-hosted and optionally federated. Each community can customize ethical frameworks while optionally sharing anonymized insights with other instances.

Closing Thoughts

"This is not just software that reflects philosophy; it's the artifact of a philosophical dialogue between human and AI. Each project is a thought experiment made concrete—a way to test whether our philosophical commitments can survive contact with real users, real infrastructure, and real constraints."

The trajectory of AI is already set—it will be used in governance, education, and civic life. The question is whether it's:

  • Extractive AI (surveillance, manipulation, control) — the default path
  • Empowering AI (deliberation, reflection, connection) — the Whetstone path

By building Sensus, The Whetstone, and Lyceum, we're making the alternative path real. We're not just critiquing dystopia—we're prototyping utopia.

And by open-sourcing it, we're ensuring that even if we fail, someone else can pick up the torch.

That's praxis. That's rebellion. That's hope.
